This section is a public study log. Everything I work through — courses, certs,
labs — gets written up here in my own words, with a link to whatever I built
along the way. This first entry is the plan itself.

## The spine: IBM Generative AI Engineering

I compared IBM's two AI professional certificates and picked **Generative AI
Engineering** over AI Engineering. The deciding factor was the next project I
want to build: a multi-model AI workflow for software development — several
models orchestrated to plan, write, and review code. That project needs
LLM-application skills (RAG, agent orchestration, deployment) far more than
model-training depth (PyTorch internals, LoRA, RLHF). The training-heavy track
isn't dropped — it's shelved as a possible later deepening once the
application-layer foundation is real.

## The side track: Security+

Alongside it, I'm working through Professor Messer's free Security+ course.
It earns its place three ways at once: it rebuilds broad IT theory from the
security angle, it carries real weight in the Saudi market, and it's a proper
entry point into security work — which I've already been doing in practice:
maintaining an attack-surface inventory for everything I deploy and
threat-modeling the AI tooling I use daily.

## The rule that makes it stick

Certificates are consumption-prone: it's easy to finish a module, collect the
checkmark, and retain nothing. So this log has one standing rule — **every
course module produces a visible artifact**: a repo, a working script, a
write-up like this one. If a module ends and there's nothing to link, the
module isn't done.

Entries land here as modules finish. The `ibm-genai` track sets the rhythm;
`security-plus` and `labs` fill in around it.
